Ideal Nature

If you've ever gone through the summary details of your Pokémon, you've likely noticed it describes their personality as "Hasty", or "Brave", or "Jolly". These terms refer to the Pokémon's Nature and actually affect the growth of a Pokémon, allowing a Pokémon to increase one of their six base stats by 10% in exchange for decreasing their stats by 10% in another stat. This is significant for competitive play to give your Pokémon an extra edge, especially when the sacrifice is for something as small as giving up 10% in an area the Pokémon wouldn't even use.


Description: A screenshot of a Chimchar's stats (from Pokémon Brilliant Diamond/Shining Pearl), showing it has a Jolly nature, as its stats show a downward arrow next to Sp. Atk and an upward arrow next to its Speed.

Nature is ordinarily random in breeding and catching wild Pokémon, but there are ways to ensure you get the nature you want. If you have a Pokémon with the ability Synchronize as the first slot in your party when looking for a Pokémon in the wild, the wild Pokémon will always have the same nature as the one with Synchronize. However, there's an even more painless way to get the ideal nature: The latest two installments in the core series, Sword & Shield and Scarlet & Violet, have mints that will change your Pokémon's nature when they consume them, so nature is one less factor you need to control in breeding/catching for competitive play!
