Starter Teams

If you're getting a taste for competitive play by taking on the challenge of the Battle Tower in Pokémon Brilliant Diamond/Shining Pearl or if you just want to get that fifth Trainer Star on your Trainer ID, you've come to the right place! Outlined below are very strong teams you can copy and follow its strategy to find success in the Battle Tower.

In the Battle Tower, you can choose to defeat 100 trainers in Singles or Doubles. In the Singles format, you and your opponent will have one Pokémon on the field at a time, and you will only have three Pokémon on your team. In the Doubles format, you and your opponent will have up to two Pokémon on the field at a time, and you will have four Pokémon on your team. You can use either format to defeat 100 trainers to get your Trainer Star! The Doubles format tends to be easier, as having two Pokémon on the field able to rely on and support each other allows some room for error for beginners.

Keep in mind that no team will have a 100% success rate in the Battle Tower due to the nature of RNG odds - sometimes, you'll just get unlucky even if you did everything right. However, these teams should get you a solid win streak as long as you've got skill and a little luck!

Ghost-Dragon Team

Format: Singles

Gengar

Ghost / Poison

Nature: Timid
Ability: Cursed Body
Held Item: Focus Sash
EVs: 4 HP / 252 SpA / 252 Spe
Moves: Dazzling Gleam, Shadow Ball, Sludge Bomb, Nasty Plot

Gyarados

Water / Flying

Nature: Adamant
Ability: Intimidate
Held Item: Lum Berry
EVs: 252 Atk / 4 SpD / 252 Spe
Moves: Crunch, Dragon Dance, Earthquake, Waterfall

Garchomp

Dragon / Ground

Nature: Jolly
Ability: Sand Veil
Held Item: Life Orb
EVs: 252 Atk / 4 SpD / 252 Spe
Moves: Outrage, Poison Jab, Rock Slide, Earthquake


Strategy: The goal here is to set up Nasty Plot with Gengar in Turn 1, then sweep the team with an appropriate move. Sludge Bomb and Shadow Ball both have STAB (Same-Type Attack Bonus) and will OHKO the opposing Pokémon 99% of the time.

If Gengar gets knocked out, Gyarados serves as a nice backup after he sets up Dragon Dance. If you’re worried about status condition cheese, you can switch out Gengar for Gyarados and Lum Berry will allow him to shake off any effect upon entering battle—just make sure you don’t switch him onto a Pokémon that knows an Electric attack.

If all else fails, Garchomp is a solid last resort, but watch out for Ice attacks. Garchomp's natural speed usually allows him to outspeed most opponents and Life Orb helps his attacks pack an even greater punch without any Sword Dance set-up.

Rain Team

Format: Doubles

Pelipper

Water / Flying

Nature: Modest
Ability: Drizzle
Held Item: Focus Sash
EVs: 252 SpA / 4 SpD / 252 Spe
Moves: Brine, Hurricane, Tailwind, Protect

Kingdra

Water / Dragon

Nature: Modest
Ability: Swift Swim
Held Item: Life Orb
EVs: 252 SpA / 4 SpD / 252 Spe
Moves: Scald, Dragon Pulse, Ice Beam, Protect

Raikou

Electric

Nature: Timid
Ability: Inner Focus
Held Item: Choice Specs
EVs: 4 Def / 252 SpA / 252 Spe
Moves: Thunder, Thunderbolt, Shadow Ball, Extrasensory

Scizor

Bug / Steel

Nature: Adamant
Ability: Technician
Held Item: Sitrus Berry
EVs: 76 HP / 252 Atk / 4 Def / 4 SpD / 172 Spe
Moves: Bullet Punch, X-Scissor, Swords Dance, Protect


Strategy: The idea is you lead with Pelipper and Kingdra and, when done right, they can sweep 90% of teams in the Battle Tower (normal mode) by themselves. Pelipper’s Drizzle ability triggers Rain when it joins battle, and Kingdra’s Swift Swift Swim ability triggers in the rain, allowing it to outspeed 99% of Pokémon in the Tower. Rain also affects moves like Hurricane (from Pelipper) and Thunder (from Raikou), giving them 100% accuracy in Rain as well as a boost to Water-type attacks.

Generally, you're going to use Hurricane with Pelipper and Scald with Kingdra for quick and easy wins. It's important to check Serebii for the oppositing team's speed stats, abilities, and moves in order to decide the best path forward. If there’s a significant threat on the other team, it's often worth it to switch out Pelipper or Kingdra for Raikou to nuke the opponent with Thunder. On the turn you switch out, use Protect on the Pokémon staying in.

It's important to note that Raikou has Choice Specs, which will lock it into the first move it uses once it's on the field - this is by design, as it's a great revenge sweeper to end a battle after a teammate goes down.

Finally, Scizor is what ties the team together in moments of need. Designed to be a bulky physical attacker, it can take a few hits (as long as they're not Fire attacks) and dish out some serious damage (often revenge damage!) thanks to its Technician ability and Bullet Punch being a priority move.
