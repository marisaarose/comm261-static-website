Training

Once you've bred your perfect Pokémon team, you'll need to get to work on maxing out their EVs. You should do this BEFORE you start levelling your Pokémon. EVs stand for Effort Values, and determine the growth for your Pokémon's stats. IVs determine the potential of that growth. If you have "Best" IVs in a stat, you've unlocked the highest potential for that stat, and now you just have to max it!

There are a couple ways you can max out your team's EVs. The easiest way is to buy vitamins, which are available at the Veilstone Department Store on the second floor. This is pretty pricey, but if you've been using the Amulet Coin in every battle since you were able to obtain it, you should have plenty of funds. If not, it's pretty quick to farm money in this game as well, especially using Game8's excellent guide.

To fully max out a single Pokémon's EVs, you'll need 53 vitamins (26 vitamins to max each preferred stat), which will cost 519,410 PokeDollars per Pokémon.

Item Purpose

Protein		 A nutritious drink for Pokémon. When consumed, it raises the Attack base points of a single Pokémon.

HP Up		 A nutritious drink for Pokémon. When consumed, it raises the HP base points of a single Pokémon.

Iron		 A nutritious drink for Pokémon. When consumed, it raises the Defense base points of a single Pokémon.

Calcium		 A nutritious drink for Pokémon. When consumed, it raises the Sp. Atk base points of a single Pokémon.

Zinc		 A nutritious drink for Pokémon. When consumed, it raises the Sp. Def base points of a single Pokémon.

Carbos		 A nutritious drink for Pokémon. When consumed, it raises the Speed base points of a single Pokémon.
If you'd rather not spend your time grinding for PokeDollars, you can also EV train by knocking out wild Pokémon using the Pokémon you're EV training. It's a good idea to use Power Items while doing this, the purpose for which is outlined on the Breeding Items page. Depending on the Power Item you use, you can increase the amount of EVs you receive from a wild Pokémon up to 8 instead of only 1 or 2. You have to be very careful about the Pokémon you knock out, though! Each species will increase EVs in a different stat. The best Pokémon to EV train against (due to EV yield and ease of finding/defeating) as well as the best place to find them are the following:
  • HP: Yields 1 EV Bidoof (Route 201) Yields 2 EVs Gastrodon (Route 218)
  • Attack: Yields 1 EV Shinx (Route 202) Yields 2 EVs Bibarel (Route 209/Route 212), Kricketune (Route 210/Route 212)
  • Defense: Yields 1 EV Geodude (Oreburgh Mine/Ruin Maniac Cave), Onix (Oreburgh Mine), Hippopotas (Ruin Maniac Cave) Yields 2 EVs Silcoon (Eterna Forest), Cascoon (Eterna Forest), Graveler (Iron Island/Mt.Coronet)
  • Special Attack: Yields 1 EV Gastly (Old Chateau) Yields 2 EVs Roselia (Route 212 North), Girafarig (Valor Lakefront), Golduck (Sendoff Spring)
  • Special Defense: Yields 1 EV Tentacool (Valley Windworks - Surf) Yields 2 EVs Tentacruel (Route 223)
  • Speed: Yields 1 EV Starly (Route 201), Magikarp (Any Route - Use Old Rod) Yields 2 EVs Floatzel (Route 218)

Once you've maxed the EVs of your team, you can work on leveling them in order to get the rest of their movepool. Pokémon in the Battle Tower will be set to level 50 no matter what level they are, but if you need a certain level for a certain move or evolution, you'll probably end up levelling your Pokémon to around that level no matter what. Quick ways to farm EXP are outlined in a guide on Game8!

Although, these days, you might be better off just sending your team to Pokémon Sword/Shield (if you have the game) and level them up using EXP candies, which are easy to rack up in Dynamax Raids. Once they're levelled, you can transfer them back to BDSP!

Once your Pokémon are trained with maxed EVs, you're ready to prepare for battle!
